# Demo Plan: Firma — 30-Minute Prospect Session (Marketing Domain)

## Context

Prospect has core Marketing domain knowledge and likely has day-to-day experience with client deliverables, campaign assets, and agency workflows. The goal is to show how Firma solves real pain points they already feel — not to explain features in the abstract. Keep it conversational and focused on "aha" moments.

---

## Session Structure

| Segment | Time | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | 0–5 min | Build rapport, frame the problem |
| Demo | 5–20 min | Show the product doing real work |
| Open Forum | 20–30 min | Hear their workflow, gather feedback |

---

## Segment 1 — Introduction (5 min max)

**Goal:** Make them feel understood before showing anything.

### Talking Points
1. **Hook with a shared pain** (1 min): "You're working with clients — delivering decks, strategies, briefs. Where do those files live? Google Drive? Dropbox? How do you share them securely, and how do you know when someone's looked at something?"
2. **Position Firma** (2 min): "Firma is a client portal that sits on top of Google Drive — your files stay where they are. We add a branded, auditable layer for sharing, permissions, and organization. Think of it as the professional front-end your Drive never had."
3. **Who it's built for** (1 min): "We built this for fractional CMOs, marketing agencies, and strategic consultants — people delivering high-touch work and juggling files across multiple clients."
4. **Set expectations** (1 min): "I'll show you a quick walkthrough, then I'd love your honest take — especially on how this fits or doesn't fit your day-to-day."

---

## Segment 2 — Demo (15 min max)

Use a pre-seeded demo firm (sandbox environment). Navigate the real app — no slides.

### Scene 1: The Firm & Client View (3 min)
- Open the firm dashboard → show the client list
- Click into a **sample client** (e.g., a marketing agency client)
- Show client status (Active), contacts, and engagement count
- **Say:** "Each client has their own space. All your work with them lives here."

### Scene 2: An Engagement in Action (3 min)
- Open an active **Engagement** inside the client
- Show the **Files tab** — documents pulled from Google Drive, no re-upload needed
- Click the comment icon on a document → show the **comment thread pane** (per-document, visible to all engagement members)
- Switch to the **Comments tab** — shows all document conversations across the engagement in one place
- **Say:** "Every document has its own comment thread — your team can discuss a brief or a deck right in context. The Comments tab gives you a single view of all open conversations across the engagement."
- _(Do NOT show Engagement Board or Wiki — these are Beta; mention at the end as roadmap items if asked)_

### Scene 3: Secure Sharing (5 min) ← KEY moment for a marketing person
- Go to the **Shares tab** on the engagement — show all externally shared documents in one place
- Click **Share** on a document — show invite with role options (Viewer/External, Contributor/External)
- Show **due dates on shared documents** — set a deadline, Firma sends auto-reminders to the client
  - _Note: Full link expiry (time-bombed links) is not yet prominently surfaced in the sharing UI — do not demo this specifically_
- Show **one-click revoke** — project ends, access ends instantly
- Show the **Audit Log** — show events like downloads, previews
  - _Note: "Opened" is only captured when the client actually previews/downloads via the portal — not from the action menu click. Frame it as: "When your client previews or downloads a file through the portal, it shows up here."_
- **"View As" moment** — use the **View As** selector in the sidebar to switch to "Viewer (External)"
  - A banner appears: "Viewing as: Viewer (External) — permissions are simulated"
  - Show exactly what the client sees — only what's been shared, no internal clutter
  - Switch back to admin view
- **Say:** "You can see exactly what your client sees before you share anything. When the engagement ends, you kill access in one click — no chasing down shared links. And if they need to act on a deliverable by Friday, Firma reminds them automatically."

### Scene 4: The Client's View (2 min)
- Switch to the client portal view (or describe it)
- Show the branded, clean interface the client sees — no Drive clutter
- **Say:** "Your client doesn't see your internal folder chaos. They see a professional portal with exactly what you've shared."

### Scene 5: Connectors (2 min) ← Critical — always show
- Show the Connectors page — Google Drive, Dropbox, Notion, Confluence, OneDrive, Box
- Show how onboarding starts with connecting a Drive
- **Say:** "Setup takes minutes — you connect your existing Drive, and your folder structure maps straight into Firma. No migration, no re-upload. If your team uses Notion or Dropbox, we connect there too."

## Segment 3 — Open Forum (10 min)

### Seed Questions (pick 2–3, don't read them all)
- "Where does this fit — or not fit — into how you actually work today?"
- "When you think about your messiest client hand-off, what would have helped?"
- "Is the Google Drive dependency a strength or a concern for your clients?"
- "What's missing that would make this a no-brainer for you?"
- "Who else on your team would need to see this before you'd consider it?"

### What to Listen For
- Pain points around file sharing chaos → validate the core pitch
- Concerns about client adoption → note for onboarding/UX improvements
- Feature gaps from their marketing workflow (campaign briefs, content calendars, approval workflows)
- Whether they think in terms of clients/engagements or something else (accounts, campaigns, retainers)

---

## Pre-Demo Checklist

- [ ] Sandbox firm has at least 2 clients, each with 1–2 active engagements
- [ ] At least 3–4 documents visible in one engagement (pulled from Google Drive)
- [ ] One shared document has a due date set (to show auto-reminder flow)
- [ ] Audit log has activity entries (previews or downloads) to show — not just opens
- [ ] Google Drive connector is already connected and healthy
- [ ] App is open and logged in before the call starts
- [ ] Test screen share before the call

---

## Key "Aha" Moments to Land

1. **Files stay in Drive** — no migration, no re-upload
2. **Due dates + auto-reminders** — clients get nudged automatically when deliverables are due
3. **Audit log** — see when your client previewed or downloaded a file
4. **One-click revoke** — clean project wrap, no chasing down shared links
5. **Branded client portal** — they see professional, not Drive chaos
